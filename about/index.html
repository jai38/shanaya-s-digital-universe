I think in choruses.

I grew up writing little melodies in the margins of textbooks. Somewhere along the way I realised I wasn't only interested in making music — I was obsessed with what it does to us. Why a sad song feels like a hug. Why a beat can change your posture.

So I trained. Voice. Piano. DJing. A cappella. Choir. Stages. Studios. Every format teaches you a different way to listen.

Now I write and perform songs that try to put words to the feelings we almost-but-not-quite have — and I curate sets that try to make rooms feel like one organism for a few hours.

— the long way here

a timeline of listening.

  1. early

    First time at a mic

    School choir — Cathedral & John Connon. Discovered that singing in a room of people made silence feel different.

  2. training

    Trinity Grade 8 · Vocal Pop & Jazz

    Plus Rock School Level 7 & 8. Years of technique so I could stop thinking about technique.

  3. piano

    Trinity Level 1 · Piano

    Because melody hits different when your hands wrote it.

  4. dj

    Pro DJ Certification · The Music Inc. / DJ Suketu

    Learning how to read a room and bend its energy with a fader.

  5. berklee

    GMI × Berklee — selected among 25 from India

    A week of being surrounded by people who think about music the way I do.

  6. now

    Originals + sets + the in-between

    Falling For You, Love Me Bold, and whatever's next.

Music that doesn't just sound meaningful— it feels meaningful.
