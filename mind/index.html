— music & mind · 05

music is felt, not just heard.

Three short studies on why a few seconds of sound can rearrange a whole afternoon.

i

— insight i

Why sad songs comfort us

A sad song isn't sadness amplified — it's sadness recognised. When the melody names what you couldn't, the lonely part of the feeling quietly leaves the room.

ii

— insight ii

Why we replay certain songs

Songs are emotional bookmarks. We don't just remember a track — we remember who we were when we first looped it. Replaying is time travel that doesn't ask permission.

iii

— insight iii

How rhythm changes mood

Before melody, before lyric, there is rhythm — and the body responds first. A slower pulse calms your breathing. A heavier beat re-shapes how you walk into a room.

The goal isn't to write songs people enjoy. It's to write songs that name something they couldn't.

Music that doesn't just sound meaningful— it feels meaningful.
